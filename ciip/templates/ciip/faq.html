What type of students does CIIP recruit?

The CIIP intern year is typically the penultimate year of a students BS or MS program. PhD students are also considered, providing business needs are met and can be provided an appropriate supervisor. Students are typically drawn from CS, EE or crossover CS/Business or IT Management university degree programs. CIIP requires students to have a passing level of English proficiency, throughtout the year students will develop their English further. We don’t only look for demonstrated academic proficiency, but excellent communication skills, combined with character attributions which suggest leadership aptitude. We want to find Cisco’s next leaders early.

What work will a CIIP intern be doing?

CIIP offers a wide variety of projects. Some CIIP placements require office work, while others require engineering/IT skills. Your internship may require a certain degree of technical skills, but a lot depends on the needs of the project given by the Hiring Manager, as well as your personal strengths. Be sure that your resume and application mentions your specific skills, experience, and interests, such as foreign language ability, extracurricular activities, leadership roles, and computer skills.

How long is the internship? Where?

The CIIP internship is a 12 month program, starting August 2014 and finishing July 2015. Depending on the project you will be involved in, you will be placed in one of the buildings of the San Jose campus.

What amenities does CIIP cover as part of its internship?

For the selected students CIIP will provide and cover the expenses:

  • A monthly salary
  • Housing
  • Work laptops
  • Return flight
  • Visa cost

Students are welcomed at SF airport and taken to their new home in San Jose upon arrival in the US.

How can I prepare for the interview?

Find out the location, what time it is and how you are getting there. If the interview is online or via phone, make sure you have no technical issues conducting the interview.

  • Go over your application form and answers to questions asked
  • Research the organization and the role, again!
  • Practice interviewing (careers service can help)
  • Prepare questions to ask the interviewer/HM
