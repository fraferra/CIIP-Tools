Frequently Asked Questions.

What kinds of students does CIIP recruit?

The CIIP intern year is typically the penultimate year of a students BS or MS program. PhD students are also considered, providing the business can provide an appropriate supervisor. Students are typically drawn from CS, EE or crossover CS/Business or IT Management kinds of university degree programs. CIIP requires a passing level of English proficiency, and students learn more fast! We don’t only look for demonstrated academic proficiency, but excellent communication skills, combined with character attributes which suggest leadership aptitude. We want to find Cisco’s next leaders early. CIIP is hugely attractive to foreign students. The best apply. We are very selective.

What work will I be doing?

CIIP offers a wide variety of projects. Some CIIP placements require office work, while others require engineering/IT skills. Your internship may require a certain degree of technical skills, but a lot depends on the needs of the project given by the Hiring Manager, as well as your personal strengths. Be sure that your resume and application mentions your specific skills, experience, and interests, such as foreign language ability, extracurricular activities, leadership roles, and computer skills.

How long is the internship? Where?

CIIP internship is 12 months in duration, starting August 2014 and finishing July 2015. Depending on the project you will be involved in, you will be placed either in San Jose or in Boston (only 6% of last year students were placed in Boston).

What does CIIP cover as part of the internship?

Students selected for a CIIP internship will receive a monthly salary, housing, work laptops, and return flights. Students are welcomed at SF airport and taken to their new home in San Jose upon arrival in the US.

How can I prepare for the interview?

- Know where you are going, what time and how you are getting there. If interview is online or via phone, make sure you have no technical issues conducting the interview. - Go over your application form and answer to questions asked - Research the organization and the role, again! - Practice interviewing (careers service can help) - Prepare questions to ask the interviewer/HM

What questions you might want to ask?

What sort of training do you offer? What are the possibilities for PT/FT employment after the internship? What are the possibilities at Cisco for using my ….. (e.g. languages, leadership skills,…)?

What type of questions I might be asked?

- Closed; questions that can be answered with only a yes or a no response; - Open; questions that demand a reasoned response from you; - Situational; we might pose a hypothetical situation to you and expect you to explain what you would do; - Behavioural; this is where we would ask you to tell us about how you acted in a past situation.
