What is the application process?

Applications close 10th January

University endorsement ends 31st January

First round of interviews finish 28th February

Second round of interviews finish 31st March

Final offers made 18th April

Visa process complete 1st July

Depart for the USA 28th July

Intern onboarding completed 8th August

What type of students does CIIP recruit?

The CIIP intern year is typically the penultimate year of a students BS or MS program. Students are typically drawn from CS, EE or crossover CS/Business or IT Management university degree programs. CIIP requires students to be proficient in the English language. The program does not only look for demonstrated academic proficiency, but excellent communication skills, combined with character attributions which suggest leadership aptitude.

What type of work will a CIIP intern be doing?

CIIP offers a wide variety of projects. Some CIIP placements require office work, while others require engineering/IT skills. Your internship may require a certain degree of technical skills, but a lot depends on the needs of projects given by the hiring manager, as well as your personal strengths. Be sure that your resume and application mention your specific skills, experience, and interests. Such as languages spoken, extracurricular activities, leadership roles, and computer/technical skills.

How long is the internship? Where?

The CIIP internship is a 12 month program, starting August 2014 and finishing July 2015. Depending on the project you will be involved in, you will be placed in one of the buildings of the San Jose campus.

What costs and amenities does CIIP cover as part of its internship?

For selected students CIIP will provide and cover the following expenses:

• A monthly salary

• Housing

• Work laptops

• Return flight

• Visa cost (you must have a visa from the origin country of your university)

• Airport transportation to housing upon arrival

How can I prepare for the interview?

Key points to consider when preparing for the interview:

1. Must make yourself avaliable to meet with Cisco managers upon request.

2. Make sure your current phone number is updated on your application.

3. Ensure you have ample amount of time for the interview.

4. If the interview is online or via phone, make sure you do not have technical issues conducting the interview.

5. Review your answers to the questions on the application form.

6. Research the organization and the role, again!

7. Practice interviewing (careers service can help).

8. Prepare questions to ask the interviewer/hiring manager.
